Pura Vida: Costa Rica

Residency & Visa Pathways

Costa Rica is highly stable and welcoming to expats. The two most popular visa pathways are:

  • **Pensionado Visa (Retiree):** Requires a lifetime, fixed income of at least **$1,000 USD** per month. You cannot be employed as a worker, but you can own a business.
  • **Rentista Visa (Passive Income):** Requires a commitment to deposit **$60,000 USD** into a Costa Rican bank (or show $2,500 USD monthly income for two years). This is ideal for those not yet retired.
  • Both paths lead to permanent residency after three years.

San Jos√© / Central Valley: The urban hub, offering the best healthcare, international schools, modern amenities, and cooler climates.

Tamarindo / Guanacaste (Pacific): Famous for its stunning beaches, surf culture, and a huge, active North American expat community. Hot and dry climate.

Dominical / Southern Pacific: Known for its lush rainforests, intense biodiversity, and a laid-back, eco-conscious lifestyle. Higher rainfall and more remote.

  • **Note:** Prices for imported goods and gasoline can be high. Local produce and services are affordable.

Global Hub: Panama

Residency & Visa Pathways

Panama is famous for its tax-friendly environment and streamlined visas. The most popular paths are:

  • **Pensionado (Retiree) Visa:** Requires a lifetime monthly income of at least **$1,000 USD** (or $1,250 if applying with a spouse). Provides significant discounts on entertainment, travel, and medical services.
  • **Friendly Nations Visa (Suspended/Modified):** Previously popular, this path has been frequently updated. We help clients navigate current investment or employment-based avenues that have replaced it.
  • **Digital Nomad Visa:** A new temporary option for remote workers, requiring a minimum monthly income of $3,000 USD.

Popular Expat Cities & Regions

Panama City: A vibrant, modern metropolitan hub with skyscrapers, excellent international banking, and world-class healthcare. Highest cost of living.

Boquete (Chiriqu√≠ Highlands): Located in the mountains, offering a mild, spring-like climate, lush greenery, and a very large, active retiree expat community.

Coronado / Pacific Beaches: A string of beach communities popular with expats, featuring gated communities, resorts, and a more relaxed lifestyle than the city.

Cost of Living Estimate (Couple)

$2,200 - $4,000 USD / month

  • **High End:** Living downtown in Panama City, using the best private hospitals and dining out often.
  • **Low End:** Living in smaller towns or outside the city core, relying more on local markets.
  • **Tax Benefit:** Panama uses a territorial tax system, meaning income earned outside Panama is generally not taxed.

Caribbean Charm: Belize

Residency & Visa Pathways

Belize is unique as the only English-speaking country in Central America. Its visa program is simple:

  • **Qualified Retired Person (QRP) Program:** Requires applicants to be **45 years or older** and prove a minimum monthly income of **$2,000 USD** (or $24,000 annually) sourced from outside Belize.
  • **Major Benefit:** QRPs are exempt from paying Belizean income tax on all foreign-sourced income and can import up to $15,000 worth of personal household goods and one vehicle duty-free.
  • **Non-QRP Residency:** After one year of continuous legal stay, one can apply for permanent residency, but the QRP program is often more attractive for retirees.

Popular Expat Cities & Regions

Ambergris Caye / San Pedro: The largest and most popular island destination, known for its diving, fishing, and large, active expat social scene. More expensive than the mainland.

Cayo District (San Ignacio): Inland region offering a lower cost of living, Maya ruins, rainforests, and farm-to-table living. Cooler than the coast.

Placencia: A peninsula with miles of beaches, offering a more relaxed, quieter coastal alternative to Ambergris Caye, with easy access to the mainland.

Cost of Living Estimate (Couple)

$1,800 - $3,500 USD / month

  • **High End:** Renting on a Caye (island), including boat taxis and imported groceries.
  • **Low End:** Living on the mainland in the Cayo District, focusing on local markets and products.
  • **Language:** Since English is the official language, the transition is often smoother for North American expats.

Common Central American Residency Visas

We specialize in these three highly popular visa categories for North American and European expats.

üí∞

Pensionado Visa

The Retiree Pathway

**Who it's for:** Retirees with guaranteed monthly income.

  • **Requirement:** Proof of a fixed, lifetime monthly income (usually minimum **$1,000 USD**).
  • **Benefit:** Allows the holder and dependents to live in the country.
  • **Key Countries:** Costa Rica, Panama.
üè¶

Rentista Visa

The Passive Income Pathway

**Who it's for:** Those with substantial savings but not yet retired.

  • **Requirement:** Must deposit a large sum (e.g., **$60,000 - $100,000 USD** in a local bank or prove equivalent monthly income for 2-5 years).
  • **Benefit:** Provides temporary residency with no minimum age requirement.
  • **Key Countries:** Costa Rica, Panama.
üå¥

Qualified Retired Person (QRP)

Belize's Expat Incentive

**Who it's for:** Retirees seeking significant tax breaks.

  • **Requirement:** Be over 45 and show **$2,000 USD** monthly income from outside Belize.
  • **Benefit:** **Tax-free income** from outside Belize and duty exemptions on household goods and a vehicle.
  • **Key Country:** Belize.
