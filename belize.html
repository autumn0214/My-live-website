Residency & Visa Pathways

Belize offers straightforward visa and residency options, including:

  • Qualified Retired Person (QRP) Program: Age 45+, $2,000/month income from outside Belize, tax exempt on foreign income, duty-free import of goods/vehicle up to $15,000
  • Permanent Residency: After one year of continuous legal stay, apply for permanent residency status
  • Temporary Employment Permit: Work permit for qualified foreign nationals sponsored by local employers
  • Student/Researcher Visa: For students attending recognized Belizean institutions
  • Dependent/Spousal Visa: For spouses or minor children of Belizean citizens/residents
  • Investor Residency: For individuals who invest in Belize businesses or property (varies by program)
  • Refugee Status: Humanitarian protection status

Popular Expat Cities & Regions

Ambergris Caye / San Pedro: The largest and most popular island destination, known for diving, fishing, and a large, active expat social scene. More expensive than mainland.

Cayo District (San Ignacio): Inland region offering a lower cost of living, Maya ruins, rainforests, and farm-to-table living. Cooler than the coast.

Placencia: A peninsula with miles of beaches, offering a relaxed coastal alternative to Ambergris Caye, with easy access to mainland.

Cost of Living Estimate (Couple)

$1,800 - $3,500 USD / month

  • High End: Renting on a Caye (island), including boat taxis and imported groceries.
  • Low End: Living on the mainland in the Cayo District, focusing on local markets and products.
  • Language: Since English is the official language, the transition is often smoother for North American expats.
