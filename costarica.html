Costa Rica is known for its biodiversity, stable democracy, and popular retiree/residency programs. Below are the practical details many expats ask about.

Residency & Visa Pathways

Most-used routes for foreigners:

  • Pensionado (Retiree): Requires a lifetime fixed income of at least $1,000 USD/mo.
  • Rentista (Passive Income): Deposit $60,000 USD into a Costa Rican bank or show $2,500 USD/month for two years.
  • Permanent residency typically after ~3 years on these programs.

Popular Expat Cities & Regions

San José / Central Valley: Best healthcare & schools.

Guanacaste / Tamarindo: Beaches & large North American expat community.

Southern Pacific (Dominical, Osa): Rainforest, remote living and ecotourism.

Practical notes

  • Public healthcare (CCSS) available to residents; strong private hospitals in San José.
  • Property ownership allowed for foreigners; check coastal servitude regulations.
  • Spanish is the primary language; English common in tourist/expat hubs.

Cost of Living Estimate (Couple)

$2,500 – $4,500 USD / month

  • High end: Modern condo in Escazú or beachfront living.
  • Low end: Local-style home outside expat centers.
