Global Hub: Panama

Residency & Visa Pathways

Panama offers a range of residency and temporary visa categories. The list below summarizes commonly used routes — check the Servicio Nacional de Migración (migracion.gob.pa) for official forms and the latest documentary requirements.

  • Pensionado (Retiree) — Proof of lifetime pension income; commonly cited minimum ~$1,000 USD/month. Provides discounts and resident benefits; documented evidence of pension required.
  • Friendly Nations / Residency (policy updated) — Historically an accelerated residency route for citizens of "friendly" countries; policy and processing have changed over time and may require a qualifying employment offer or investment — consult the official Panama migration pages for current exact requirements.
  • Investment / Business (Inversionista) — Residency via qualifying investment in business or real estate. Minimum thresholds and acceptable investment types vary; official guidance and supporting documentation required.
  • Work / Employment — Employer-sponsored work permits and temporary residency for specialized personnel. Employer petitions to migration authorities and Social Security (CSS) registration are typically required.
  • Digital Nomad / Remote Worker — Panama has introduced temporary visas/permits for remote workers or digital nomads; income thresholds and documentation differ by program — check official government announcements for the current scheme and eligibility (proof of remote employment, income, insurance, etc.).
  • Student — For international students enrolled in recognized Panamanian institutions; requires enrollment confirmation and proof of financial means for the period of study.
  • Family / Marriage — Residency avenues for spouses and dependent children of Panamanian citizens or residents; supporting civil documents and translations/apostilles usually required.
  • Permanent Residency — May follow multiple years of qualifying temporary residency or be available through family ties; specific timelines and documentation are set by migration rules.

Practical requirements & process notes

  • Common documentation: apostilled birth/marriage certificates, criminal background checks (no older than specified by migration), proof of income or pension, bank statements, and authenticated translations where needed.
  • Many residence categories require in-country steps (appointments, document verifications, fingerprinting) — use Servicio Nacional de Migración (migracion.gob.pa) and official consular guidance.

Popular Expat Cities & Regions

Panama City: The country's modern hub with international banking, best private healthcare, and highest connectivity.

Boquete (Chiriquí Highlands): Mountain town with mild climate and a large retiree community.

Coronado / Pacific Beaches: Popular beach communities offering gated developments and relaxed coastal living.

Healthcare, Internet & Practical Notes

  • Private hospitals in Panama City provide high-quality specialist care; private insurance costs vary (est. $70–$220+/month depending on coverage and age).
  • Internet: typical urban residential speeds often range 40–150 Mbps.
  • Territorial tax system — Panama generally taxes Panama-sourced income; foreign-sourced income can be exempt depending on structure and current regulation.

Cost of Living Estimate (Couple)

$2,200 - $4,000 USD / month

  • High end: Panama City downtown living with private hospitals and frequent dining out.
  • Low end: Smaller towns or outside the city core relying on local markets.
  • Note: Territorial tax rules can favor some foreign income; consult an advisor.
