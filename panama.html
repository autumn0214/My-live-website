Destination Guide

Global Hub: Panama

Residency & Visa Pathways

Panama is famous for its tax-friendly environment and streamlined visas. The most popular paths are:

  • Pensionado (Retiree) Visa: Requires a lifetime monthly income of at least $1,000 USD (or $1,250 if applying with a spouse). Provides significant discounts on entertainment, travel, and medical services.
  • Friendly Nations Visa (Suspended/Modified): Previously popular, this path has been frequently updated. We help clients navigate current investment or employment-based avenues that have replaced it.
  • Digital Nomad Visa: A new temporary option for remote workers, requiring a minimum monthly income of $3,000 USD.

Popular Expat Cities & Regions

Panama City: A vibrant, modern metropolitan hub with skyscrapers, excellent international banking, and world-class healthcare. Highest cost of living.

Boquete (Chiriquí Highlands): Located in the mountains, offering a mild, spring-like climate, lush greenery, and a very large, active retiree expat community.

Coronado / Pacific Beaches: A string of beach communities popular with expats, featuring gated communities, resorts, and a more relaxed lifestyle than the city.

Cost of Living Estimate (Couple)

$2,200 - $4,000 USD / month

  • High End: Living downtown in Panama City, using the best private hospitals and dining out often.
  • Low End: Living in smaller towns or outside the city core, relying more on local markets.
  • Tax Benefit: Panama uses a territorial tax system, meaning income earned outside Panama is generally not taxed.
