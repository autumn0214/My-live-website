Residency & Visa Pathways

Panama has one of Latin America's most diverse offering of residency visas:

  • Pensionado (Retiree) Visa: Proof of $1,000 monthly income from pension
  • Friendly Nations Visa: For citizens of eligible countries; requires investment (property or local company), or new employment within Panama
  • Qualified Investor Visa: Direct investment of $300,000+ USD into Panama real estate, financial assets, or business
  • Self-Economic Solvency Visa: For those with $300,000+ in bank or investment (real estate or fixed deposit)
  • Professional Employment Visa: For university graduates who find work with a Panamanian business
  • Marriage/Family Visa: For spouses or dependent children of Panamanian citizens or residents
  • Digital Nomad Visa: Temporary residency for remote workers earning $3,000+/month outside Panama
  • Student Visa: For students admitted to accredited Panamanian institutions
  • Refugee/Asylee Status: For those granted humanitarian protection
  • Forestry Investor Visa: Investment in reforestation, starting ~$100,000 USD

Popular Expat Cities & Regions

Panama City: A vibrant, modern metropolitan hub with skyscrapers, excellent international banking, and world-class healthcare. Highest cost of living.

Boquete (Chiriquí Highlands): Located in the mountains, offering a mild, spring-like climate, lush greenery, and a very large, active retiree expat community.

Coronado / Pacific Beaches: A string of beach communities popular with expats, featuring gated communities, resorts, and a more relaxed lifestyle than the city.

Cost of Living Estimate (Couple)

$2,200 - $4,000 USD / month

  • High End: Living downtown in Panama City, using the best private hospitals and dining out often.
  • Low End: Living in smaller towns or outside the city core, relying more on local markets.
  • Tax Benefit: Panama uses a territorial tax system, meaning income earned outside Panama is generally not taxed.
